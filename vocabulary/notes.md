## 2. Complex Sentences

### 2.1 Sentence Transformation

**Basic:**
I will call Sarah to fix the pipes at my place. She is an expert in that and she can help me.

**Advanced:**
Since Sarah is an expert in fixing pipes, I will call her so she can fix the ones at my place.

---

### 2.2 Useful Connectors

Since, Considering, Concerning, Due to, Because of, Not only, Given that, While, Although, Based on

---

### 2.3 Example

**Basic:**
I was told briefly about the problems in the apartment but I didn’t know it was that bad.

**Advanced:**
Although I was briefly told about the problems in the apartment, I didn’t know it was that bad.

---

## 3. Punctuation

### 3.1 Commas

**Extra info:**
I, Bob Baker, hereby declare you President.  
Due to the ongoing strikes, after the President’s resignation, we will have to shut down schools tomorrow.  
Gary Smith, professor at the University of Toronto, will join us later.

**Connectors:**
I am okay with the winters in Canada, however, this year I have had enough!  
The chickens have stopped producing eggs, therefore, we will buy new chickens.

---

### 3.2 Semicolons

**Conclusion:**
I read the book; from now on, I will use those lessons.

**Connector:**
Home prices are rising; moreover, inflation affects groceries.

**Adding idea:**
I fear flying; I prefer driving.

**Incorrect:**
I love cakes; I will fly tomorrow.  
There are plants; which grow in summer.

---

## 5. Sentence Upgrade

**Basic:**
Employees are rude.

**Advanced:**
The older employees at my local office are rude and communicate inefficiently.

---

## 6. Connectors

**Addition:** Furthermore, Moreover, Also, Plus  
**Contrast:** However, Nevertheless, Although  
**Conclusion:** Therefore, Hence, Overall  

---

## 7. Strategy

Use a combination of:
- Advanced vocabulary  
- Connectors  
- Complex sentences  
- Proper punctuation  

Goal: clear and well-structured long sentences.
